FAQ

Everything your board
will ask. Answered.

Most organizations can't answer the board's ROI question because AI spend, initiative outcomes, and business KPIs live in completely different systems. TowerIQ connects them into one view — linking AI assets to strategic initiatives, tracking KPI measurements (hours saved, tickets deflected, revenue influenced), and translating domain metrics into dollar values your CFO can put in a board packet.

The result: instead of "we deployed 14 AI tools this quarter," you can say "our AI portfolio generated $2.8M in measurable value against $1.1M in combined spend — a 2.5× return, with 3 underperforming tools flagged for consolidation."
"The board doesn't want to hear about model accuracy. They want to know if the $4M we spent on AI last year moved the needle." — VP of Technology, Fortune 500 Insurer
TowerIQ maps your AI portfolio against the major global frameworks — out of the box, with no manual configuration required:

EU AI Act ISO 42001 NIST AI RMF ISO 27001 SR 11-7 (Model Risk) FDA AI/ML Guidance US Executive Order on AI HIPAA AI Alignment

Every asset is automatically classified by risk tier (EU AI Act categories), lifecycle stage, and control coverage. When a regulator asks for evidence, TowerIQ generates an audit-ready evidence pack — DPIA documentation, bias assessments, committee approvals, and decision history — in one click.

The common thread across every regulation: you must know what AI you have, where it's deployed, what it decides, and who owns it. That inventory is table stakes — and it's where TowerIQ starts.
Two reasons — one external, one internal.

External: The EU AI Act is not a future concern. Phase 2 obligations apply from August 2025. Full high-risk AI enforcement begins August 2026. Organizations that start their AI inventory today have roughly 12–16 months to build compliance. Independent assessments show it takes 3–6 months to inventory a mid-size enterprise AI portfolio manually. That window is not as wide as it looks.

Internal: Every month you wait, your AI footprint grows — and so does the remediation cost. Discovering 47 ungoverned AI tools is a manageable problem. Discovering 200, with production models making customer-facing decisions, is an emergency. The earlier you establish the foundation, the cheaper and easier governance becomes.
"Starting late doesn't mean doing less work. It means doing the same work under deadline pressure, with regulators watching." — CISO, European Financial Services Group
General-purpose GRC tools were designed for IT risk management — not for the AI era. They require manual asset registration, don't understand LLM cost models, can't auto-discover a new Salesforce AgentForce deployment or a shadow AI tool an employee signed up for last Tuesday, and have no concept of model drift, token spend, or hallucination risk.

TowerIQ is not a replacement for your GRC platform — it's the AI-specific intelligence layer that feeds it. We auto-discover what exists, classify risk, track lifecycle, and export compliance evidence. Your GRC platform handles the broader enterprise risk posture. Together, they're complete.
Organizations consistently underestimate their AI footprint by 3 to 5 times. When TowerIQ connects to your identity provider and development platforms, it routinely surfaces tools that IT has no record of — AI embedded in SaaS apps, developer-provisioned LLM keys, copilot tools enabled by default in Microsoft 365, browser extensions making AI API calls with corporate credentials.

If you think you have 15 tools, you likely have 40–70. The organizations that discover this early have options: consolidate, govern, or retire. Those that discover it during a regulatory audit have one option — fix it under scrutiny.

Smaller footprints are also the easiest moment to start. Building governance muscle now, before AI proliferates, is dramatically cheaper than retrofitting it later.
TowerIQ connects in minutes, not months. There is no SDK to instrument, no agents to deploy, no data residency changes required.

We've designed TowerIQ specifically for technology leaders who don't have 6 months to spare. The complexity is ours to manage — you get the outcomes.
Beyond regulatory fines, the compounding costs of inaction are often overlooked:

Shadow AI accumulates. Every month, more employees adopt ungoverned AI tools. By the time you audit, some will be handling customer data, making automated decisions, or exposing confidential IP to third-party model providers — without your knowledge.

Wasted spend compounds. Duplicate tools across departments, unused licenses, API costs with no attribution — IDC estimates $4.1M in average annual AI spend waste per enterprise. Every quarter without visibility is another quarter of that waste.

Remediation gets exponentially harder. A 50-asset portfolio is manageable. A 300-asset portfolio with 2 years of ungoverned history — some in production, some abandoned, some with no identifiable owner — is a months-long remediation project.

Board credibility erodes. When the next audit, the next M&A due diligence, or the next regulator asks for your AI inventory, "we're working on it" is not an answer that inspires confidence.
